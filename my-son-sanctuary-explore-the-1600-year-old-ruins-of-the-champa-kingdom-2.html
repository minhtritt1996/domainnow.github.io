My Son Sanctuary: Explore the 1600-Year-Old Ruins of the Champa Kingdom

A visit to My Son Sanctuary will add a memorable experience to your trip to Hoi An or Da Nang. This UNESCO World Heritage Site offers a glimpse into the past of the ancient Champa Kingdom, showcasing breathtaking ruins and architectural wonders.

My Son Sanctuary - A UNESCO World Heritage Site In December 1999, UNESCO recognized My Son Sanctuary as a World Heritage Site. This Cham tower complex is one of Vietnam's most beautiful and impressive Cham temples. Each temple showcases a unique structural style that reflects the historical stages of the Champa kingdoms. Only about 20 of these temple towers remain at My Son due to damage from natural disasters and war. However, it continues to be a popular destination that attracts tourists from around the world.

1. Where is My Son Sanctuary in Vietnam?

My Son Sanctuary comprises several ancient Cham temples spread across a 2-kilometer-wide valley surrounded by hills and mountains. Though time and war have taken their toll on these relics, what remains of My Son plays an important role in world history and culture.

1.2. How to Get to My Son Sanctuary?

My Son is approximately 69 kilometers from Da Nang and 45 kilometers from Hoi An Ancient Town. You can reach My Son from either city.

From Hoi An:

  1. By Bike: Take a scooter from Hoi An, follow Tran Hung Dao Street, cross Vinh Dien River, and continue straight to Quang Trung street. Signs will guide you to My Son.
  2. By Bus: Public buses run from Hoi An to My Son, but they do not stop at the entrance. Alternatively, you can reserve a shuttle bus that departs at 8:30 AM and returns at 1:45 PM for around $8.
  3. By Car: Car rentals in Hoi An are limited. If you're renting a car, expect to travel from Da Nang Airport with a rental costing about $100 for the day.

From Da Nang:

  1. By Bike or Car: Follow National Highway 1 to Nam Phuoc Town (39 km), turn onto Route 537, and follow signs to My Son. Continue on the mountain road for about 5 minutes.
  2. By Bus: Bus number 06 departs from Da Nang every 30 minutes, starting at 5:30 AM until 5 PM, with fares ranging from 8,000 to 30,000 VND.

1.3. Map of My Son Sanctuary

A detailed map is available at the entrance of My Son Sanctuary to help you navigate the site and locate key attractions.

2. History of My Son Sanctuary in Quang Nam

My Son Sanctuary was built between the 4th and 13th centuries CE by the Cham people, a culture that developed along the coast of modern Vietnam and had spiritual origins in Indian Hinduism. The impressive tower temples at My Son served as the political and religious capital of the Champa Kingdom. The stone stele of the Cham royal dynasties confirms My Son's importance as the sacred heart of the Cham people during those centuries.

3. Highlights of My Son Sanctuary

The sanctuary spans 1158 hectares. It takes about 2 to 3 hours to explore its most notable features.

3.1. Ancient Cham Architecture of My Son Sanctuary

The most captivating aspect of My Son Sanctuary is its unique Cham architecture, heavily influenced by Indian styles. The Cham towers were built on quadrilateral foundations, and each tower has three parts:

  • A sturdy foundation representing the mortal world.
  • A sacred body symbolizing the spiritual world.
  • The tower top, shaped like a man offering flowers, birds, or animals, representing the connection between spirits and humans.

Though many structures have been damaged, the remaining towers and sculptures offer a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of the Cham people.

3.2. My Son Sanctuary Museum

After entering the sanctuary, visitors should first explore the museum. The museum houses exhibits on My Son's relics and history, providing valuable context for understanding the siteâ€™s significance. Be aware that the museum closes half an hour earlier than the sanctuary.

3.3. Inspiring Apsara Dance in My Son Sanctuary

In addition to its architectural beauty, My Son offers daily performances of traditional Cham dances. Local dancers perform a variety of dances, from the iconic Shiva and Apsara dances to joyful fan and water dances. These performances, accompanied by folk music, enhance the sacred atmosphere of the sanctuary.

3.4. Kate Festival in My Son Sanctuary

Every October, the Cham people celebrate the Kate festival, or Mbang Kate, for three days. This event is the Cham peopleâ€™s most significant celebration, honoring their heroes and ancestors. The festival features vibrant traditional costumes, passionate dances, and symphonic music, creating a surreal cultural experience.

4. Must-Try Food Near My Son Sanctuary

While visiting My Son, be sure to try some of Central Vietnam's most iconic dishes, such as:

  • Quang Noodles (Mi Quang)
  • Cau Mong Smoked Calf (Be Thui Cau Mong)
  • Phu Chiem Noodles (Mi Phu Chiem)
  • Vietnamese Pancakes (Banh Xeo)
  • Rice Crackers (Banh Trang Dap)

There are a few restaurants at the entrance to My Son and eateries in nearby towns where you can sample these delicacies.

5. Where to Stay for Your Visit to My Son Sanctuary?

Accommodation options near My Son Sanctuary are limited, so visitors are advised to stay in Hoi An or Da Nang. Hotels and resorts in these areas offer convenient access to My Son and other attractions.

For a perfect family vacation with cultural experiences, consider staying at Vinpearl Resort & Golf Nam Hoi An, located on Binh Minh beach, just a 20-minute drive from Hoi An. Guests can also enjoy the nearby amusement park, VinWonders Nam Hoi An.

My Son Sanctuary remains a key destination for history lovers and those seeking to immerse themselves in the Cham culture of Vietnam.
